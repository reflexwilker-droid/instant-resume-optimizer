Resume Tips That Get Hired

✨ Start with Strong Action Verbs

Instead of "Responsible for managing a team," write "Led a team of 5 engineers." Use words like Led, Built, Increased, Reduced, Launched, Optimized.

📈 Quantify Your Achievements

Numbers catch the eye. "Boosted sales by 30%" or "Cut processing time from 10 hours to 2" are far more impactful than vague statements.

🔑 Tailor for ATS

Many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems. Include keywords from the job description. Match exact phrases they use (e.g., "Agile methodologies", "customer retention").

📄 Keep It Concise

Aim for one page if you have less than 10 years experience; two pages max for senior roles. Use clean formatting and a readable font (10-12pt).

🎯 Customize for Each Job

Generic resumes get ignored. Reorder your bullet points to highlight the most relevant experience for the specific role you're applying to.

📧 Proofread Ruthlessly

Typos and grammatical errors are instant deal-breakers. Run spell check, then read aloud. Ask a friend to review.

💼 Include a Professional Summary

Replace outdated "Objective" with a 2-3 line "Professional Summary" at the top that highlights your years of experience, key skills, and what you bring to the role.

🛠️ Use a Simple, Standard Format

Avoid graphics, tables, icons, or fancy templates. They can break in ATS. Use standard headings (Experience, Education, Skills) and plain text for maximum compatibility.
